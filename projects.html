Rapid Response Drone Mapping and Tracking: Saving Lives Today Not Tomorrow

When disaster strikes, drones can play invaluable roles in providing first responders with situational awareness, mapping the damage and establishing target areas for disaster relief, providing an effective communication with coordinated response plan, and aiding in search-and-rescue efforts to identify survivors and recover the lost.

Through our experience in daily use of drones for urban scanning and mapping we have found that it requires the development of specific hardware, software and infrastructure to ensure autonomous and safe use of drones and to maximize their efficiency in helping and not getting in the way of first responders during any disaster relief effort. We developed a rapid response system with 4 phases of drone integration to provide new kinds of services that can be practically and economically implemented to improve disaster relief dramatically.

Congested Urban Scanning: Al-Balad Cultural Heritage

Al-Balad is the old souk found in Jeddah’s ancient seaport founded in the 7th century C.E.. It served as the gate to Makkah and remains a center of commerce and trade within Jeddah. Al-Balad, recently recognised as an UNESCO cultural heritage site, provides a new drive to preserve this rapidly decaying symbol of Hijazi culture. As a contribution to this effort, we have conducted an extensive Aerial and Terrestrial 3D scan to digitally document its buildings. The 250,000m² of the UNESCO site was captured in ca. 70 minutes of flight time producing over 3.000 images.

Bab Gadeed - Jeddah Old Town
(Al-Balad Cultural Heritage)

We have a team of CAD Engineers that can convert any 3D scan into a survey-grade CAD model within only a couple days. Our approach to final model generation diverges from the common practice of point cloud meshing, which we believe introduces significant errors where holes and gaps are filled, adds unnecessary file size, overloads computer rendering and is no closer to an As-built CAD solid/wireframe. In contrast, we export our scanned point clouds into standard file formats that can be accessed by Autodesk’s AutoCAD 2014, RIVET or 3DS Max depending on the demand of our customers. We semi-automatically model the buildings using the measurements of the point cloud, enabling us to preserve the minute structural details of the buildings. Using the point cloud as a reference significantly speeds up the process and at the same time constricts the model to the actual intricate dimensions of the original building. Since all measurements are accurate to 1cm, final As-built plans can be generated for any part of the point cloud from the original SfM scan.

Bait Al-batarjy - Jeddah Old Town
(Al-Balad Cultural Heritage)

Bait Al-batrajy is a historic building in Al-Balad that once served as the U.S. Embassy. This model demonstrates the level of detail that can be achieved by modeling from our 3D scans. Our CAD Engineers were asked to model every component of the building exterior: from the beams of the intricately hand-carved rawashin projecting latticed windows to the modern air-conditioning units and tv satellites the reconstruction captures the smallest detail. Since we could scan the building from multiple-angles using our multirotors and merge the aerial scans with terrestrial laser scans, our CAD engineers had detailed measurements of the entire building including the latticed windows and other complicated occluding elements. The result is a highly detailed building survey model that can not only be used for documentation but dropped right into any visualization software or game engine.

Al-Balad Virtual Museum Application
(Al-Balad Cultural Heritage)

Visualisation of our aerial scanned assets is a key service that differentiates FalconViz from most survey and mapping companies. Our talented team includes software developers focused on developing bespoke applications to help our customers visualize and promote their most valuable assets. One product we are particularly excited about is our Virtual Museum Application which allows us to convert any area surveyed and mapped into an open air digital museum. This tool provides our customers an interactive building timeline. Users are able to step through different time elements of the building and its multiple layers of information. For Al-Balad it allows users to interactively swipe through the significant historical events of Al-Balad’s buildings, their construction, and conservation/renovation. As Al-Balad is now a UNESCO cultural heritage site this tool will allow the global community to track the current progress of its conservation over the next several years. We are currently demonstrating our application in the Bayt Nasif Cultural Heritage museum in Al-Balad, Jeddah.

Mada’n Saleh and Al-Ula

We had the unique opportunity in 2013 to conduct a preliminary aerial scan of the archaeological monuments and excavations in Mada’n Saleh and Al-Ula. This was part of a joint archaeological and computer vision project initiated by KAUST, KSU, and KACST to digitally capture the ruins and excavations of Ancient Dedan in Saudi Arabia. Aerial scanning’s contribution to the archaeological field is its ability to achieve perspectives of architecture not possible from ground scanning. Many cultural heritage sites and especially archaeological excavations contain a high level of occlusion. Occlusion is detrimental to scanning because anything that is concealed by another object is not scanned. It results in large gaps or holes in the data that can only be resolved by conducting more scans from different locations. The advantage of our scanning method is that we can generate 3D mission plans that can capture complex areas from many angles producing a complete scanned model of an area.

NEO (New Energy Oasis) Park - KAUST

The New Energy Oasis is a solar panel park where many different companies’ solar energy systems are tested for their power efficiency. FalconViz was asked to provide aerial images and video for the park. Besides this, our CAD Engineers created a CAD model with accurate measurements of all solar panels based on the point cloud reference. Additionally, we created stunning aerial images for the whole location at different times of the day which will be used to analyze how the sun's location at different times of the day and year affect panel placement.

Makkah Historical Aqueduct
"Ayn Zubaydah"

Ayn Zubaydah has remained a source of fresh water to Makkah and nearby holy sites for the last 1,200 years. It is a masterpiece of engineering and an enduring symbol of the golden era of the Arab culture. In light of the great concern of the Saudi government for preservation and restoration of such a historical place, we have been assigned to propose a complete survey and scan. As a pilot study a complete survey of one of its Aqueducts was conducted using both our aerial scanning system and ground based laser scanning. Our CAD engineers uses the resulting point cloud to create a CAD model with accurate measurement to facilitate the restoration process as well as preserving the value of such asset to the future generations.

KSA Roadway Project

We scanned a 2km section of Kholais road running through the mountainous region near Makkah. Aerial scans are generated by our autonomous UAV’s flying pre-planned transects at elevations between 70m and 100m, well below any regulated air space. In addition, the low elevation allows ground sampling densities between 1-3.5cm per pixel in comparison to the latest satellite imagery of 25-75cm per pixel. The autonomous flights can be adjusted to cover massive stretches of land, road and/or railway that can extend beyond 100km.

3D Fabrication of KAUST Beacon

Being one of the most recognisable land marks for King Abdullah University of Science and technology, the KAUST Beacon rises 60 meters above the water and is comprised of amorphous hexagonal sections arranged in an elliptical shape. We scanned this structure by air with our copters and by water with a boat in order to generate a full 3d model with accurate measurements. This model was then plugged into our software to semi-automatically detect the hexagonal planes of the architecture and generate a solid AutoCAD model.
